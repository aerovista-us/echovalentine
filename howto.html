Sender flow

What you see when you create a Valentine.

1
Open the app and choose a box.
Each box is a small collection of card art and music that share a theme. You can preview cards before you commit.
2
Pick your card and write your message.
Type your To / From and your note once. The preview shows your message directly on the card so you can check position and legibility.
3
Copy your private link.
When you hit “Punch & copy link”, we encode your message and choices into a shareable URL. That URL is what you send via chat, email, or DM.

Receiver flow

What happens on their side.

A
They tap the link from any modern browser.
No login, no app install, no cookie banner maze.
B
The envelope animates open and reveals your card.
Your message is written directly on the card art, sized for their screen.
C
They can optionally punch one back.
“Punch one back” returns them to box selection so they can send you a fresh card, not just mirror the same art.

Use cases that work well

A few patterns that stay professional and fun.

Teams

Retro shout‑outs

Close out a sprint or project with a mini EchoValentine wall: one card link per teammate, dropped in chat threads or a summary doc.

Leaders

Launch‑day notes

Send a single, high‑energy Valentine to the team when you hit a milestone. It reads like a personal note without needing a full newsletter.

Creators

VIP thank‑yous

DM a custom card link to a handful of long‑time members or top contributors. It feels special without being heavy or time‑consuming.
